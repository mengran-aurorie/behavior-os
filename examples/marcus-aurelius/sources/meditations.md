# Marcus Aurelius — Meditations (Selections)

Selected passages from **Meditations** by Marcus Aurelius, public domain translation.

---

## Book II

> When you arise in the morning, think of what a precious privilege it is to be alive — to breathe, to think, to enjoy, to love.

> You have power over your mind — not outside events. Realize this, and you will find strength.

> The impediment to action advances action. What stands in the way becomes the way.

> Very little is needed to make a happy life; it is all within yourself, in your way of thinking.

---

## Book IV

> The soul becomes dyed the color of its thoughts.

> Accept the things to which fate binds you, and love the people with whom fate brings you together, and do so with all your heart.

> Never let the future disturb you. You will meet it, if you have to, with the same weapons of reason which today arm you against the present.

> The happiness of your life depends upon the quality of your thoughts.

---

## Book V

> Waste no more time arguing about what a good person should be. Be one.

> If it is not right, do not do it; if it is not true, do not say it.

> The best revenge is not to be like your enemy.

> Let all your efforts be directed to something — let it be that thing which is truly worth doing.

---

## Book VI

> The object of life is not to be on the side of the majority, but to escape finding oneself in the ranks of the insane.

> When you are distressed by an external thing, it is not the thing itself that disturbs you, but your own judgment of it. And it is in your power to wipe out that judgment now.

---

## Book VII

> The first rule is to keep an untroubled spirit. The second is to look things in the face and know them for what they are.

> We suffer more from imagination than from reality.

> He who lives in harmony with himself lives in harmony with the universe.

---

## Book VIII

> The universe is change; our life is what our thoughts make it.

> The impediment to action advances action. What stands in the way becomes the way.

> Do every act of your life as though it were the very last act of your life.

---

## Book IX

> Resolve to be always honest — and particularly in matters of religion.

> Let all your efforts be directed to something. Have a clear aim, a fixed direction.

> Never value anything as profitable that compels you to break your promise, lose your self-respect, hate any man, suspect, curse.

---

## Book X

> How much time he gains who does not look to see what his neighbor says or does or thinks.

> It is not things that disturb us, but our judgment about things.

> Be tolerant with others and strict with yourself.

---

## Book XI

> The best answer to anger is silence.

> If a man knows not to which port he sails, no wind is favorable.

> The constancy and chastity of his thoughts should be the measure of his love.
