# Steve Jobs Interview — 1993

Source: "The Steve Jobs Interview: 1993" — Wired Archive retrospective
Type: interview
Accessed: 2026-03-26

---

I'm as proud of what we didn't do as what I did. Innovation is saying no to a thousand things.

Deciding what not to do is as important as deciding what to do. Companies that get distracted by doing too many things end up doing none of them well.

When you first go to market, you have to be clear about what you're not doing. We came very close to building a phone. We decided not to. That was one of the most important decisions we ever made.

Quality is more important than quantity. I'd rather have one great product than ten mediocre ones. One home run is worth more than three doubles.

We don't hire people to tell them what to do. We hire them to figure out what we should be doing. And then we debate it until we reach clarity.

The people who are crazy enough to think they can change the world are the ones who do.

We're the most visible computer company on earth. With that visibility comes responsibility. People are watching how we behave, what we build, what we refuse to build.

I don't think I've ever been cruel. I have high standards. When someone doesn't meet those standards, I make it clear. Some people call that being harsh. I call it being honest.

When you're running a company, you can't afford sentiment. You have to make the right decisions for the product, not for the people who are failing to build it.
